## I. THE CORE PROBLEM: five layers of distortion

the early buddhist texts are read today through five layers of distortion, each imposed at a different historical moment. decolonizing means peeling all five:

| layer | when imposed | by whom | what it does | example |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| 1. translation | 19th c. onward | pali text society, max muller, rhys davids | maps pali terms onto english words carrying christian/enlightenment metaphysics | nibbana→"extinction", dukkha→"suffering", saddha→"faith" |
| 2. categorization | 19th c. onward | protestant orientalists, "science of religion" | forces buddhism into the category "religion" (a protestant construct) and assigns it a position on the monotheism/polytheism/atheism axis | "buddhism is an atheistic religion" |
| 3. periodization | colonial + modern | european historical frameworks | imposes greco-roman periodization (ancient/medieval/modern) onto indian traditions that don't follow it | "early buddhism" as proto-protestant rationalism, "later buddhism" as degenerate superstition |
| 4. linguistic imperialism | 19th c. → present | anglophone academy | makes english the medium of buddhist studies, giving english-language categories gatekeeping power over what buddhism "means" | the entire field's literature is in english; asian-language scholarship is "secondary" |
| 5. internalization | 19th c. → present | colonized elites, asian modernist reformers | asian buddhists themselves adopt western categories, creating "protestant buddhism" | dharmapala, buddhadasa, mindfulness movement |

## II. CHECKLIST: colonial categories to reject

### A. terms that import abrahamic metaphysics

| term used | what it imports | what the pali actually says | replacement |
|-----------|----------------|---------------------------|-------------|
| "suffering" for dukkha | christian vale of tears, suffering as punishment | unsatisfactoriness, dis-ease, unreliability of conditioned phenomena | use "dukkha" untranslated, gloss as "the unreliable, unsatisfactory character of conditioned experience" |
| "no-self" / "no-soul" for anatta | negation of christian "soul" (created, immortal, judged) | not-self as ADJECTIVE applied in practice: "this is not mine, this is not I, this is not my self" — a tool, not a metaphysical claim | use "anatta" untranslated; distinguish "not-self" (adjective, therapeutic) from "no-self" (noun, ontological) — the whole thesis of ch1 |
| "extinction" for nibbana | nihilism, annihilation of being | quenching of the fires of greed, hatred, delusion; MN 72 fire simile shows the question "where does it go?" is grammatically wrong | use "nibbana" untranslated; gloss as "quenching" or "unbinding" |
| "faith" for saddha | blind belief, fideism, submission to authority | shraddha/saddha = "holding the mind aligned with truth" (srat + dha); the kalama sutta explicitly rejects blind faith | use "saddha" untranslated; gloss as "confidence born from investigation" |
| "meditation" for jhana/bhavana | generic relaxation, prayer, contemplation | jhana = four precisely defined experiential states with specific phenomenological markers; bhavana = cultivation/development (not sitting quietly) | use "jhana" and "bhavana" untranslated; specify which jhana, what is being cultivated |
| "salvation" for vimutti/nibbana | external savior, grace, atonement, judgment day | liberation through one's own practice; no external agent | use "vimutti" (release) untranslated |
| "morality" / "ethics" for sila | kantian duty, utilitarian calculus, divine command theory | sila = training rules undertaken voluntarily as part of the path; connected to karma, consciousness, liberation — not to an external moral law | use "sila" untranslated; gloss as "training in conduct" |
| "concentration" for samadhi | mental focus, willpower, narrowing attention | samadhi = unification, composure, heightened super-consciousness beyond normal cognition; NOT diminished/narrowed awareness | use "samadhi" untranslated; gloss as "unification of mind" |
| "formations" for sankhara | vague, meaningless abstract noun | sankhara = volitional activities, conditioned phenomena, the constructing process itself; one of the five khandhas AND a link in dependent origination AND a general term — each usage is precise | use "sankhara" untranslated; always specify which sense |
| "religion" for dhamma/sasana | private belief system, faith community, creed | dhamma = "that which upholds/supports" — encompasses natural law, cosmic order, teaching, practice, truth; sasana = dispensation, instruction | use "dhamma" and "sasana" untranslated |
| "atheism" for the buddha's position | denial of god within the monotheism/atheism binary (an abrahamic axis) | three-layer response: (1) functional refutation of creator-theology (kills effort), (2) therapeutic deferral of metaphysical questions, (3) pragmatic retention of devas/brahma within samsara. the axis is "does this reduce dukkha?" not "does god exist?" | NEVER call the buddha an atheist. describe the three layers. this is the whole thesis of ch5 |
| "philosophy" for the buddha's teaching | greek-derived discipline of abstract reasoning about concepts | the buddha taught a PATH (magga) — practice instructions for ending dukkha. the early register is method-shaped, not thesis-shaped | prefer "teaching" or "dhamma" to "philosophy"; the method-shape IS the point of the book |
| "mind" for citta/mano/vinnana | cartesian mind (thinking substance, private theater) | three distinct pali terms with different referents: citta (heart-mind, the affective center), mano (thinking faculty), vinnana (discriminative consciousness, cognizing) | use the pali terms; never collapse all three into "mind" |

### B. structural categories to reject

| category | why it distorts | what to use instead |
|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| "buddhism" as a "religion" | imposes the protestant model: founder, creed, scripture, clergy, faith, salvation. the buddha didn't found a "religion" — he taught a practice. | "the buddha's dhamma" or "the sasana" — a dispensation, a training, a path |
| "early buddhism" vs. "later buddhism" as linear decline | mirrors protestant narrative of pure origin → corrupt church → reformation. the real drift is method→thesis, not pure→corrupt | use "stratum" language: "earliest stratum texts," "early stratum," "abhidhamma stratum." describe WHAT changes (register, collocational environment, function) not WHETHER it gets worse |
| the theravada/mahayana divide as fundamental | a product of 19th c. orientalist taxonomy. the early texts were shared across all schools | focus on TEXTUAL STRATA and PARALLEL VERSIONS (pali + chinese agama), not school labels |
| "canon" / "scripture" for tipitaka | imports the bible model: single authoritative text dictated by divine authority | use "tipitaka" (three baskets) — an anthology, not a single text; multiple recensions across schools |
| "monk" / "nun" for bhikkhu/bhikkhuni | imports christian monastic model: vows of poverty, cloistered, contemplative | use bhikkhu/bhikkhuni — almsmen/almswomen living a specific vinaya-governed life |
| the "atheist/theist" axis itself | an abrahamic taxonomic axis that has no native pali equivalent. natthika is a term of abuse in the canon, applied to nihilists — never to the buddha | use the buddha's own axis: "does this serve the ending of dukkha?" |

### C. scholarly frameworks to reject

| framework | what's wrong with it | source |
|-----------|---------------------|--------|
| muller's "disease of language" | mythology = linguistic degeneration of an original monotheism. applied to the vedas to recover a proto-christian "unknown god." the entire "science of religion" was protestant theology in disguise | yelle, language of disenchantment |
| "protestant buddhism" as reform | dharmapala et al. internalized colonial categories: textualism, anti-ritual, rationalism, individual meditation. this is NOT decolonization — it is deeper colonization | elst, decolonizing the hindu mind |
| the "mindfulness movement" as authentic buddhism | strips sati from its eightfold path context, removes sila and samadhi, turns method into product. this IS the method→thesis drift happening in real time | directly relevant to ch3 |
| asoka = buddhist emperor promoting buddhism | olivelle dismantles this: asoka's dharma deliberately omits rebirth, karma, nibbana. he created a supra-religious civil religion. buddhism was one pasanda among several | olivelle in empires and religion |
| the five ELT fallacies (transposed to buddhist studies) | (1) buddhism best discussed in english, (2) western-trained PhDs over tradition-trained bhikkhus, (3) learn western philosophy first then "apply" to buddhism, (4) more english secondary literature is always better, (5) too much pali = not enough "rigor" | phillipson, linguistic imperialism |

## IV. FAULT LINES: where the colonial framework cracks

these are the specific points where the colonial/orientalist/protestant reading of early buddhism breaks down under textual scrutiny. each one is a potential argument:

### fault line 1: the grammar of anatta
- colonial reading: "there is no self" (metaphysical negation)
- textual reality: "this is not mine, this is not I, this is not my self" (practical de-identification)
- the grammar is ADJECTIVE in the earliest strata, NOUN in the later strata
- evidence: ch1 H index tracks this drift quantitatively

### fault line 2: the fire simile's dissolution
- colonial reading: nibbana = "extinction" (nihilism) or "salvation" (escape to another realm)
- textual reality: MN 72 — the question "where does the fire go when extinguished?" is grammatically wrong. nibbana is not a place, not a state, not an entity — it's the cessation of the conditions for burning
- evidence: ch4 should track the collocational environment of nibbana across strata

### fault line 3: the issara/issariya split
- colonial reading: the buddha rejects "god" (single position on the theist/atheist axis)
- textual reality: TWO different terms. issaranimmana (creator-theology) = ALWAYS rejected. issariya (sovereignty/mastery) = positive or neutral. even the SAME WORD (vasavatti) is rejected for brahma (who claims to be creator) and accepted for the tathagata (who is sovereign but not creator)
- evidence: ch5 term dictionary, corpus data

### fault line 4: the natthika trap
- colonial reading: buddha = ancient atheist, precursor of rational secularism
- textual reality: natthika appears 49x in the canon, EVERY TIME as a label for wrong view. the buddha explicitly teaches the OPPOSITE of natthikavada: "there IS giving, there IS sacrifice, there IS fruit of good and bad actions"
- evidence: ch5 corpus scan

### fault line 5: the method→thesis drift IS the colonial drift
- colonial reading: "later buddhism" = corruption/degeneration (protestant narrative of pure origins)
- textual reality: the drift is from METHOD-SHAPE (practice instructions, doing-verbs, similes, second-person address) to THESIS-SHAPE (definitions, classifications, third-person analysis). this is not degeneration — it is a CHANGE IN REGISTER. but the same change happens again when western scholars convert method-shaped texts into thesis-shaped scholarship
- evidence: ch3 MHI tracks this quantitatively

### fault line 6: asoka's deliberate omission
- colonial reading: asoka = buddhist emperor promoting buddhism as state religion
- textual reality: asoka's dharma deliberately omits rebirth, karma, nibbana, three of the five precepts. he created a SUPRA-RELIGIOUS civil religion. buddhism was one pasanda among four
- evidence: olivelle's analysis in empires and religion

### fault line 7: the vedic engagement before opposition
- colonial reading: buddha = reformer who rejected hinduism (protestant reformation narrative projected onto india)
- textual reality: early strata ENGAGE vedic concepts (redefining brahman, tapas, yajna, karma), not rejecting them. the opposition-stance hardens in later strata
- evidence: ch2 SI tracks engagement→opposition quantitatively

### fault line 8: the saddha mistranslation
- colonial reading: buddhism is a "faith tradition" (because saddha = faith)
- textual reality: saddha etymologically = "holding the mind aligned with truth" (srat + dha). the kalama sutta explicitly rejects blind faith. translating saddha as "faith" makes buddhism look like christianity
- evidence: malhotra's analysis of shraddha, pali corpus usage

### fault line 9: the canonization as method→thesis operation
- colonial reading: the pali text society "preserved" buddhism by creating a critical edition of the "canon"
- textual reality: the act of canonization ITSELF was a method→thesis operation: converting oral, practice-embedded, context-dependent teaching into a fixed, printed, translated, scholarly TEXT. the PTS (1881, london) did to the tipitaka what muller did to the rig veda — protestantized it
- evidence: yelle on muller + phillipson on publishing imperialism

### fault line 10: the language determines what can be thought
- colonial reading: english is a "neutral" medium for studying buddhism
- textual reality: english-language categories (religion, philosophy, mind, consciousness, self, meditation, suffering, extinction) carry judeo-christian + enlightenment metaphysics. they are NOT neutral. the medium distorts the message. "the language determines what CAN be thought about the tradition" (phillipson)
- evidence: all 8 texts converge on this point

## V. THE BOOK'S POSITION: how to write from the 5th BCE mindset

the ab-initio project's position, synthesized from all of the above:

1. **use pali terms untranslated** for all core concepts. provide glosses, not translations. a gloss explains; a translation replaces.

2. **track the drift quantitatively** — this is what makes the project different from yet another opinion piece. H, SI, MHI are EVIDENCE that the register changes. the colonial critics can dismiss opinions; they cannot dismiss frequency counts.

3. **name the layers of distortion explicitly** — every chapter should acknowledge: "this concept is normally understood through [colonial translation X]. the textual evidence shows [Y instead]."

4. **the method-shape IS the buddha's voice.** the earliest strata speak in second person, use doing-verbs, deploy similes, address the practitioner. the thesis-shape speaks in third person, uses definitions, deploys taxonomies, addresses the scholar. recovering the method-shape is recovering the voice.

5. **the three-axis framework replaces the two-axis one:**
   - colonial axis: theist / atheist (abrahamic binary)
   - actual axis 1: does this reduce dukkha? (soteriological)
   - actual axis 2: does this serve as method or thesis? (register)
   - actual axis 3: does this engage or oppose? (stance)

6. **the decolonization chapter (ch6) is not an appendix — it is the meta-argument** that explains WHY the drift matters beyond the texts themselves. the same method→thesis transformation that happened within the tradition (5th BCE → 5th CE) happened AGAIN when the tradition was absorbed into western scholarship (19th CE → present).
