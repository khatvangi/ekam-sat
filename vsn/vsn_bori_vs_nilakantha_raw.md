# Viṣṇu Sahasranāma: BORI CE vs Nīlakaṇṭha (vulgate)

## Source texts
- BORI CE: Anuśāsanaparva 13.135.014-120 (Harvard-Kyoto)
- Nīlakaṇṭha: Bhāratabhāvadīpa, vol. 9000, pages 154-158 (Surya OCR, ~80% quality)
- Nīlakaṇṭha's chapter numbering: 149 (= BORI 135)

## Important note from Nīlakaṇṭha
Nīlakaṇṭha explicitly states that for the individual name meanings,
one should consult Śaṅkarācārya's bhāṣya:
*śrīśaṅkarācāryebhagavatpādīyabhāṣyādevāvagantavyaḥ*

## Key observations

### OCR quality issues in Nīlakaṇṭha text
The Surya OCR at ~80% quality produces systematic errors:
- 'bh' misread: भूत → सूत (bhUta → sUta) in verse 14
- 'pr' misread: प्रभुः → प्रशः (prabhuH → prazaH) in verse 14
- Word boundaries lost: continuous text without spaces
- Devanagari conjuncts misread consistently

### Genuine textual variants (pending verification)
Cannot reliably distinguish OCR errors from genuine variants at 80% accuracy.
Need either:
1. Higher quality OCR (Gemini) of vol. 9000 pages 154-158
2. Chitrashala press edition (physical text) for the vulgate reading
3. Śaṅkara's bhāṣya text (not available locally — web sources returning 404s)

### What Nīlakaṇṭha DOES say
1. The VS names are mostly self-explanatory (*spaṣṭārthaḥ*)
2. For etymological analysis of individual names, consult Śaṅkara's bhāṣya
3. Even śūdras have adhikāra (right) to recite the VS — *śūdraḥ sukham avāpnuyāt*
4. The VS is complementary to the Śiva Sahasranāma in the Śāntiparva
   (Nīla notes that the Śiva SN has 1,004 names vs VS's ~1,000)
