### The Word RĀMA — Four Roots

| Root | Derivation | Theology |
|------|-----------|----------|
| √ram (to delight) | rāma = "the one in whom all delight" | ĀNANDA — the divine is bliss |
| √ram (to rest) | rāma = "the one in whom the mind rests" | ŚĀNTI — the divine is peace |
| √rā (to give) | rā + ma = "giving + to me" | KṚPĀ — the divine is grace |
| √rañj (to color) | rāma = "the dark one" | KṚṢṆA — rāma and kṛṣṇa share etymology |

### The Syllables rā + ma

- rā + ma = giving + to me — the complete circuit of grace
- rā + ma = giving + Lakṣmī — the bestower of prosperity
- rā + ma = giving + not — the one who gives even non-being (mokṣa)
- rā + ma = giving + measure — the one who gives the unmeasurable
