# The Rāma Verse: Textual History and Source Comparison

## Three Sources, Three Versions

### 1. Padma Purāṇa (Uttarakhaṇḍa 71.331) — THE ORIGINAL

**Context:** Umāpati-Nārada saṃvāda (Śiva speaks to Nārada)

> रामरामेति रामेति रमे रामे मनोरमे ।
> सहस्रनामतत्तुल्यं रामनाम वरानने ॥

> rāmarāmeti rāmeti rame rāme manorame |
> sahasranāmatattulyaṃ rāmanāma varānane ||

> రామరామేతి రామేతి రమే రామే మనోరమే |
> సహస్రనామతత్తుల్యం రామనామ వరాననే ||

Spaceless (line 1): rāmarāmetirāmetiramerāmemanorame (32 chars)

**Features:** No śrī prefix. rāmeti appears TWICE. "rāma — thus, rāma — thus" = two discrete acts of naming.

### 2. VS Vulgate (phala-śruti v.27) — THE ADAPTATION

**Context:** Pārvatī-Īśvara saṃvāda (Pārvatī asks, Śiva answers)

> श्रीरामरामरामेति रमे रामे मनोरमे ।
> सहस्रनामतत्तुल्यं रामनाम वरानने ॥

> śrīrāmarāmarāmeti rame rāme manorame |
> sahasranāmatattulyaṃ rāmanāma varānane ||

> శ్రీరామరామరామేతి రమే రామే మనోరమే |
> సహస్రనామతత్తుల్యం రామనామ వరాననే ||

Spaceless (line 1): śrīrāmarāmarāmetiramerāmemanorame (33 chars)

Variant reading noted: (rāmarāmeti rāmeti) — this MATCHES the Padma Purāṇa text.

**Changes from Padma Purāṇa:**
- Added śrī at the beginning (Vaiṣṇava devotional addition)
- Changed "rāmeti rāmeti" to "rāma rāma rāmeti" (two quotations → triple invocation)
- Changed addressee from Nārada to Pārvatī

### 3. BORI Critical Edition — ABSENT

The verse does not appear anywhere in the BORI CE's 18 parvas (~79,290 verses). The BORI editors excised the entire Pārvatī-Śiva dialogue from the VS phala-śruti. The BORI phala-śruti (13.135.121-142) is spoken by Bhīṣma to Yudhiṣṭhira only.

---

## Evidence for Priority of the Padma Purāṇa Version

1. **The VS vulgate's own variant reading matches the Padma text.** The editors who compiled the vulgate knew the Padma version and recorded it as "(rāmarāmeti rāmeti)." The variant IS the Padma original.

2. **Lectio brevior.** The shorter version (no śrī, 32 chars) is typically considered prior. The longer version (with śrī, 33 chars) is an expansion.

3. **Structural argument.** The Padma version's "rāmeti rāmeti" (iti twice) preserves the quotation structure: each "rāmeti" = "rāma — iti" = one act of naming. Two acts. The VS version's "rāma rāma rāmeti" converts this into triple repetition with a single iti. The quotation structure is more archaic; the repetition structure is a devotional smoothing.

4. **Dialogue frame.** Śiva-to-Nārada is standard Purāṇic pedagogy. Śiva-to-Pārvatī (with Pārvatī as questioner) is a later device frequently used to insert Tāntric or Śaiva material into existing texts.

5. **BORI excision.** The BORI editors independently judged this material as interpolation, confirming it was a later addition to the MBH text.

---

## The rame/rāme Distinction

ALL three sources agree on the second pāda: **rame rāme manorame**

- **rame** (रमे, short a) = √ram, 1st person ātmanepada: "I delight"
- **rāme** (रामे, long ā) = locative of rāma: "in Rāma"

The verse says: "I delight (rame) in Rāma (rāme), O mind-delighter (manorame)."

The devotee's voice (rame = I) and the deity's name (rāme = in Rāma) coexist in the same pāda. This is stable across all versions.

## Impact on the Splitting Analysis

Three strings need separate analysis:

| Version | String | Chars |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Padma Purāṇa | rāmarāmetirāmetiramerāmemanorame | 32 |
| VS Vulgate | śrīrāmarāmarāmetiramerāmemanorame | 33 |
| Traditional recitation* | śrīrāmarāmetirāmerāmemanorame | 29 |

*The "traditional recitation" form used in the ayamatma essay is neither the Padma nor the VS vulgate exactly — it appears to be a further simplification in oral tradition (one rāma dropped, rāme for rame).

The essay at `/storage/ayamatma/rama/srirama_nivedana_final.md` uses the 29-char form. It should note the textual variants and indicate which string is being analyzed.
