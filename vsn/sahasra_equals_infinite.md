No human being, no tradition, no commentarial lineage can exhaust this space. Every commentary that has ever been written on the VS — Śaṅkara's, Parāśara Bhaṭṭa's, Nīlakaṇṭha's, and every other — is a single point in a space of 10^2,103.

## Sahasra

The word *sahasra* in Vedic Sanskrit does not only mean the number 1,000. It is used throughout the Ṛgveda and the Brāhmaṇas to mean "innumerable" — a finite word gesturing toward the infinite. *Sahasraśīrṣā puruṣaḥ* (RV 10.90.1) — "the Puruṣa of a thousand heads" — does not mean the cosmic person has exactly 1,000 heads. It means the heads are beyond counting.

The Viṣṇu Sahasranāma is sahasra in both senses:
1. **Literally**: you CAN extract exactly 1,000 names (the traditional commentators do)
2. **Vedically**: the text contains beyond-counting theological readings (~10^2,103)

"One thousand names" is the finite gesture. The text behind it is infinite.

## The Three-Level Structure

**Level 1 — Splitting**: determines HOW MANY attributes the divine has
- More splits = more distinction (the divine has many separate functions)
- Fewer splits = more unity (the divine's functions are indivisible)
- The VS verse 14 distribution is perfectly symmetric: 1,7,19,26,19,7,1

**Level 2 — Etymology**: determines WHAT each attribute means
- Same word, different root = different theology
- Example: bhāva from √bhū (Being) vs √bhā (Luminosity) — same letters, different gods
- 82,944 combinations for just the 9 traditional names

**Level 3 — Combined**: the full theological space
- Each path = a complete darśana (philosophical view)
- The tradition picks one path. The text contains all of them.
- That is what makes it scripture and not just text.
