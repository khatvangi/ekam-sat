# śrīrāma rāmeti rāme rāme manorame
## A Rāmanavamī Offering: The Mathematics of the Divine Name

---

### The Verse

**Devanāgarī:** श्रीराम रामेति रामे रामे मनोरमे
**Telugu:** శ్రీరామ రామేతి రామే రామే మనోరమే
**IAST:** śrīrāma rāmeti rāme rāme manorame

Spoken by Śiva to Pārvatī in the Viṣṇu Sahasranāma phala-śruti:

> śrīrāma rāmeti rāme rāme manorame |
> sahasranāma tattulyaṃ rāmanāma varānane ||

"O beautiful one — 'Rāma, Rāma' — this Rāma-name is equal to the entire thousand names."

### The Discovery

29 characters. 13 internal split points. The distribution of valid readings:

| Segments | Readings | = C(13,k) |
|----------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | 1 | C(13,0) |

| 13 | 13 | C(13,12) |
| 14 | 1 | C(13,13) |
| **Total** | **8,192** | **= 2^13** |

**This is Pascal's Triangle, row 13.** The distribution is EXACTLY the binomial coefficients. The verse is a perfect binary system: 13 independent switches, each on or off, producing 2^13 = 8,192 readings.

No other verse we analyzed (VS verse 14, Śiva SN verse 150) has this property. Those verses have constraints — minimum segment lengths that prevent adjacent splits. This verse is constructed so that EVERY split point is independent of every other. The verse is **combinatorially free**.

This is not an accident. The verse consists almost entirely of repetitions of rā-me, which means every 2-character boundary is a valid split. The ṛṣis built the most mathematically perfect verse possible: a string where every possible reading is valid.

### Seven Readings — Seven Paths to the Divine

#### 1. The Bhakti Reading (devotional)
**श्री | राम | रामेति | रामे | रामे | मनोरमे**

"O Śrī! Rāma! Rāma! — thus chanting, in Rāma, in Rāma, O mind-delighting one..."

Pure devotion. The devotee calls the name and delights in the calling. The traditional reading.

#### 2. The Jñāna Reading (knowledge)
**श्री | राम | रामे | इति | रामे | रामे | मनो | रमे**

"Śrī Rāma delights — thus knowing, in Rāma, in Rāma, the mind delights."

Knowing that Rāma delights, the mind attains delight. Knowledge produces bliss.

#### 3. The Ātman Reading (the divine within)
**श्री | राम | रामे | इति | रामे | रामे | मनोरमे**

"Śrī Rāma delights-in-me — thus, in Rāma, in Rāma, O mind-charmer."

*rame* = "in me." Rāma delights in dwelling within the devotee. The delight is mutual. The divine is not distant — it delights in YOU.

#### 4. The Lakṣmī Reading (feminine divine)
**श्री | रामा | रामा | इति | रामे | रामे | मनोरमे**

"Śrī! Rāmā! Rāmā! — thus, in delight, in delight, O mind-charmer."

*rāmā* (feminine) = the beautiful one = Sītā = Lakṣmī. Same letters, different gender, different deity. The verse invokes the GODDESS, not the god.

#### 5. The Līlā Reading (divine play)
**श्री | राम | रामेति | रामे | रामे | मनो | रमे**

"O Śrī Rāma! — thus, in Rāma's delight, in Rāma's delight, the mind plays."

√ram = to play, to sport. The mind plays in the delight that IS Rāma. Creation as divine play.

#### 6. The Mantra Reading (seed-syllables)
**श्री | रा | म | रा | मे | ति | रा | मे | रा | मे | म | नो | र | मे**

14 segments. Each syllable a bīja (seed):
- रा (*rā*) = √rā, to give, to bestow
- म (*ma*) = the Lakṣmī-bīja, the feminine
- मे (*me*) = to me, for me
- नो (*no*) = to us, for us
- र (*ra*) = the fire-bīja

The verse is not a sentence but a garland of seed-syllables, each carrying its own śakti. This is the tāntric reading.

#### 7. The Single-Word Reading (absolute unity)
**श्रीरामरामेतिरामेरामेमनोरमे**

1 segment. The entire verse is one word. One breath. One name. The divine is so unified that even the distinction between "Rāma" and "delight" and "mind" collapses. There is only the one vibration.

### The Śiva-Rāma Connection

Śiva says this verse. Śiva — who in the Mahābhārata has his OWN sahasranāma (chapter 13.014) — tells his wife that one name of Viṣṇu equals all thousand. Our analysis of the Śiva Sahasranāma (verse 150) shows 271.8 billion readings. Our analysis of the Viṣṇu Sahasranāma shows 10^2,103 readings.

And this single half-verse — 29 characters — contains 8,192 readings, with the mathematically perfect structure of Pascal's Triangle.

The claim "one name equals a thousand" is not poetic exaggeration. Every sufficiently long Sanskrit string contains an astronomical number of valid readings. The name Rāma, repeated in a verse designed for combinatorial freedom, contains its own infinity.

### rā + ma

Two syllables. The complete circuit:

**रा** (*rā*) = √rā — to give, to bestow
**म** (*ma*) = to me / the feminine / Lakṣmī

"The one who gives" + "to me" = the complete circuit of grace.

Or: "The one who gives" + "the feminine principle" = Rāma and Sītā in two syllables.

Or: "Bestowing" + "Lakṣmī" = the source of all auspiciousness.

The name you chant is not one word. It is a universe of words, compressed into two syllables.

On Śrī Rāmanavamī, this is worth knowing: the tradition that says "Rāma" equals all names is not speaking in metaphor. It is speaking in mathematics. The Sanskrit was designed to hold all readings simultaneously. That is what makes it mantra and not just sound.

**8,192 readings. Pascal's Triangle. 2^13. From 29 characters.**

*Sahasra* was always the right word.
