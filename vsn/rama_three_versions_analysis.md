# The Rāma Verse: Three Versions, Three Mountains, One Invariant

## All Three Are Pascal's Triangle

| Version | Source | Chars | Splits | Readings | Row | Peak |
|---------|--------|-------|--------|----------|-----|------|
| Padma Purāṇa | Uttarakhaṇḍa 71.331 | 32 | 15 | 32,768 | Row 15 | 8-9 (twin) |
| VS Vulgate | phala-śruti v.27 | 33 | 15 | 32,768 | Row 15 | 8-9 (twin) |
| Traditional recitation | common prayer books | 29 | 13 | 8,192 | Row 13 | 7-8 (twin) |

**The Pascal property is INVARIANT.** It survives adding śrī, changing rāmeti×2 to rāma×3, dropping a rāma. The uniform 2-character spacing is preserved in every version because every version is built from the same rā-me phonemic substrate.

## The Strings

**Padma Purāṇa (32 chars, 15 splits, 2^15 = 32,768):**
```
रामरामेतिरामेतिरमेरामेमनोरमे
rāmarāmetirāmetiramerāmemanorame
```

**VS Vulgate (33 chars, 15 splits, 2^15 = 32,768):**
```
श्रीरामरामरामेतिरमेरामेमनोरमे
śrīrāmarāmarāmetiramerāmemanorame
```

**Traditional (29 chars, 13 splits, 2^13 = 8,192):**
```
श्रीरामरामेतिरामेरामेमनोरमे
śrīrāmarāmetirāmerāmemanorame
```

## How the Hidden Words Shift

| Finding | Padma (32) | Vulgate (33) | Trad. (29) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **mar** (death) | 1× | **2×** (doubled — extra rāma+rāma junction) | 1× |
| **ram** (delight) | 2× | 2× | 1× |
| **eti** (approaches) | **2×** (doubled — rāmeti appears twice) | 1× | 1× |
| **tirā** (crossing) | 1× | **0** (lost — iti+rame junction, not iti+rāme) | 1× |
| **irā** (Sarasvatī) | 1× | **0** (lost — same reason) | 1× |
| Unique hidden words | 5 of 5 | 3 of 5 | 5 of 5 |

## What Each Version Gains and Loses

### Padma Purāṇa (the original): most architecturally elegant
- **Doubled eti mirrors doubled rāmeti.** The surface says "rāma-thus, rāma-thus" (two acts of naming). The boundary hides "he-approaches, he-approaches" (two acts of coming). The quotation structure and the hidden verb have the same count.
- All 5 hidden words present. The most complete version.
- 32,768 readings.

### VS Vulgate (the adaptation): more death, less crossing
- **Gains a second mar** — the extra rāma creates rāma+rāma+rāmeti, producing m-a-r at TWO junctions. More death to reverse.
- **Loses tirā and irā** — the "rāmeti rāmeti" → "rāma rāma rāmeti" change eliminated the second iti-rā junction. No crossing-word, no speech-goddess at that boundary.
- The added śrī adds 3 chars but no new split points (śrī has only one vowel-consonant boundary, at the same 2-char spacing).
- 32,768 readings — same as Padma despite being 1 char longer.

### Traditional recitation (simplified): most diverse per character
- All 5 hidden words, each exactly once.
- Fewest readings (8,192) but richest DIVERSITY of hidden words relative to string length.
- 5 hidden words in 29 chars = 1 per 5.8 chars.
- The version most people actually chant.

## The Invariant

The Pascal property holds because the rā-me substrate is the same in all three versions. The verse, in every form the tradition has transmitted it, is built from 2-character units that happen to be valid Sanskrit morphemes. This makes every split point independent. Independence produces the binomial distribution. The binomial distribution IS Meru Prastāra.

No editorial modification — adding a syllable, removing a syllable, changing the quotation structure — can break this property as long as the rā-me substrate is preserved. The tradition, across centuries of textual evolution, preserved exactly this.

That is the deepest finding: **the invariant the tradition preserved is not the verse's exact wording. It is the verse's combinatorial freedom.**
