## Key Findings

### 1. The Rāma verse is UNIQUE
It is the only verse analyzed that produces EXACT Pascal's Triangle (2^13 = 8,192). No other verse — not in the VS, not in the Śiva SN — has this property. The rā-me repetition creates perfectly independent split points.

### 2. The Śiva SN verse 153 (śukla) has the most readings per character
1.57 trillion readings from 76 characters. The fivefold repetition of śukla- creates massive combinatorial space. But it is NOT Pascal — the longer compound words (śuklāmbaradharāya, śuklabhasmāvaliptāya) create constraints.

### 3. Verse 155 (ṛṣabhas tvaṃ) dwarfs everything
3.6 × 10^19 readings from 122 characters. This verse — "you are the bull among the pure, Śiva among yogins, the householder among āśramas, Maheśvara among lords, Kubera among yakṣas, Viṣṇu among sacrifices" — has the richest combinatorial structure because it lists MANY short names in parallel.

### 4. The Śiva SN is combinatorially denser than the VS
VS verse 14 (88 chars, 55 splits) → 54.5 billion readings
Śiva SN verse 150 (71 chars, 46 splits) → 271.8 billion readings
**Shorter verse, 5× more readings.** The dative -āya endings create more split opportunities.

### 5. None of the Śiva SN verses are Pascal
The Rāma verse's Pascal property comes from equal spacing of split points (every 2 characters). The Śiva SN verses have irregular spacing due to long compounds (mahādevāya, vajrahastāya, śuklāmbaradharāya), which create dependencies between adjacent splits.

## The Theological Implication

The Rāma verse is designed for MAXIMUM FREEDOM — every possible reading is equally accessible. This is consistent with Śiva's claim that the Rāma-name equals the entire sahasranāma: the verse is engineered to contain the most readings in the least space.

The Śiva SN verses are designed for MAXIMUM CONTENT — long, specific compounds (vajrahasta, pinākapāṇi, śuklāmbaradhara) pack theological meaning into each name, at the cost of combinatorial freedom.

Two design philosophies:
- **Rāma verse**: compress the SPACE of meanings (freedom over content)
- **Śiva SN**: compress the CONTENT of meanings (content over freedom)

Both are infinite. The Rāma verse's infinity is pure and symmetric (Pascal). The Śiva SN's infinity is rich and constrained (shaped by the specific attributes of Śiva).
