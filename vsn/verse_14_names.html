Reading 1: Nine Names

Maximum Splitting — Each Divine Function is Distinct

#DevanāgarīTeluguIASTEnglish
1विश्वंవిశ్వంviśvaṃThe Universe / The All-pervading
2विष्णुःవిష్ణుఃviṣṇuḥThe Pervader (vi + √ṣ, to pervade)
3वषट्कारःవషట్కారఃvaṣaṭkāraḥThe Embodiment of the sacrificial call Vaṣaṭ
4भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुःభూతభవ్యభవత్ప్రభుఃbhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥThe Lord of past, future, and present
5भूतकृत्భూతకృత్bhūtakṛtThe Creator of all beings
6भूतभृत्భూతభృత్bhūtabhṛtThe Sustainer of all beings
7भावःభావఃbhāvaḥExistence itself / Pure Being
8भूतात्माభూతాత్మాbhūtātmāThe Self (Ātman) of all beings
9भूतभावनःభూతభావనఃbhūtabhāvanaḥThe Cause of all beings' becoming

This is BORI's reading and Śaṅkara's tradition. It is the unique maximum — the only decomposition that yields 9 names. Every pair that can be split is split.

Reading 2: Seven Names

One Compound Merged — Creation and Sustenance as One Act

#DevanāgarīTeluguIASTEnglish
1विश्वंవిశ్వంviśvaṃThe Universe
2विष्णुःవిష్ణుఃviṣṇuḥThe Pervader
3वषट्कारःవషట్కారఃvaṣaṭkāraḥThe Vaṣaṭ Embodiment
4भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुःభూతభవ్యభవత్ప్రభుఃbhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥLord of past, future, present
5भूतकृद्भूतभृद्भावःభూతకృద్భూతభృద్భావఃbhūtakṛdbhūtabhṛdbhāvaḥCreator-Sustainer-Existence (triple dvandva)
6भूतात्माభూతాత్మాbhūtātmāThe Self of all beings
7भूतभावनःభూతభావనఃbhūtabhāvanaḥThe Cause of becoming

Name 5 merges bhūtakṛt (creator), bhūtabhṛt (sustainer), and bhāva (existence) into a single triple dvandva compound. The theological implication: creation, sustenance, and existence are not three separate divine functions but one undivided act.

Reading 3: Three Names

Maximally Monistic — ALL Functions as ONE

विश्वंविष्णुः విశ్వంవిష్ణుః 1. The All-pervading Viṣṇu
वषट्कारोभूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः వషట్కారోభూతభవ్యభవత్ప్రభుః 2. The Vaṣaṭ who is Lord of all times
भूतकृद्भूतभृद्भावोभूतात्माभूतभावनः భూతకృద్భూతభృద్భావోభూతాత్మాభూతభావనః 3. Creator-Sustainer-Existence-Self-Cause — ALL as ONE

The third name is the mega-compound: creator-sustainer-existence-self-cause — five divine functions collapsed into a single unbroken word. This is Advaita in grammar. The divine does not have five attributes. The divine is one reality that appears as five when you split the compound.
