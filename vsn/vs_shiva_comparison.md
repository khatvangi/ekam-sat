# Compound Splitting: VS Verse 14 vs Śiva SN Verse 150

## The Discovery

The same principle operates in BOTH sahasranāmas, but through different grammatical means.

## VS Verse 14 (Viṣṇu — Nominative epithets)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Characters | 88 |
| Valid decompositions | 80 |
| Range | 3 to 9 names |
| Distribution | 1, 7, 19, **26**, 19, 7, 1 |
| Shape | **Perfectly symmetric** (bell curve) |
| BORI reading | 9 names (unique maximum) |
| Theological axis | More splits = more distinction within the divine |

## Śiva SN Verse 150 (Śiva — Dative salutations)

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Characters | 71 |
| Valid decompositions | 72 |
| Range | 4 to 10 names |
| Distribution | 2, 9, 18, **21**, 15, 6, 1 |
| Shape | **Nearly symmetric** (slight right skew) |
| BORI reading | 9 names (one of 6 readings at that count) |
| Theological axis | More splits = more acts of worship |

## Key Differences

### 1. Case determines the theological question

- **VS (nominative)**: "viśvaṃ viṣṇuḥ vaṣaṭkāraḥ..." — these ARE the divine. The splitting asks: how many attributes does the divine have?
- **Śiva SN (dative)**: "namo devādhidevāya mahādevāya..." — these are offered TO the divine. The splitting asks: how many salutations does the devotee make?

### 2. The VS maximum is unique; the Śiva SN maximum splits a WORD

- VS: the 9-name reading is the ONLY decomposition at that count. It is the unique maximum.
- Śiva SN: the 10-name reading splits mahādevāya into mahā + devāya — breaking a WORD, not just a compound. This is grammatically possible (mahā is a valid prefix) but theologically violent: it says Śiva is not the Great God (mahādeva) but merely "great" (mahā) and "a god" (deva) separately.

### 3. Symmetry

- VS: perfectly symmetric (1,7,19,26,19,7,1). The combinatorics of nominative compounds produce a clean bell curve.
- Śiva SN: nearly symmetric (2,9,18,21,15,6,1) but with slight right skew. The dative -āya endings create more split opportunities on one side.

### 4. BORI's position in the distribution

- VS: BORI chose the unique maximum (9 names). Every possible split taken.
- Śiva SN: BORI chose 9 names — but this is NOT the maximum (10 exists). BORI stops short of splitting mahādeva.

## The Shared Principle

In both texts: **more splitting = more distinction; less splitting = more unity.**
In both texts: **the text holds all readings simultaneously.**
In both texts: **the commentator's choice of splitting IS the theology.**

The difference is that the VS encodes this through nominative compound-formation (what the divine IS) and the Śiva SN encodes it through dative salutation (what the devotee OFFERS). Same mathematical structure. Different ritual grammar.
