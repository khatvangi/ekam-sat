# Viṣṇu Sahasranāma — Verse 14: The First Nine Names

## The Verse

**IAST:**
> viśvaṃ viṣṇur vaṣaṭkāro bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ |
> bhūtakṛd bhūtabhṛd bhāvo bhūtātmā bhūtabhāvanaḥ ||

**Devanāgarī:**
> विश्वं विष्णुर् वषट्कारो भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः ।
> भूतकृद् भूतभृद् भावो भूतात्मा भूतभावनः ॥

**Telugu:**
> విశ్వం విష్ణుర్ వషట్కారో భూతభవ్యభవత్ప్రభుః |
> భూతకృద్ భూతభృద్ భావో భూతాత్మా భూతభావనః ||

---

## Reading 1: Nine Names (BORI / Śaṅkara — Maximum Splitting)

Each divine function is a distinct name. Creation ≠ sustenance ≠ existence ≠ self ≠ cause.

| # | IAST | Devanāgarī | Telugu | English |
|---|------|------------|--------|---------|
| 1 | viśvaṃ | विश्वं | విశ్వం | The Universe / The All-pervading |
| 2 | viṣṇuḥ | विष्णुः | విష్ణుః | The Pervader (vi + √ṣ, to pervade) |
| 3 | vaṣaṭkāraḥ | वषट्कारः | వషట్కారః | The Embodiment of the sacrificial call Vaṣaṭ |
| 4 | bhūtabhavyabhavatprabhuḥ | भूतभव्यभवत्प्रभुः | భూతభవ్యభవత్ప్రభుః | The Lord of past, future, and present |
| 5 | bhūtakṛt | भूतकृत् | భూతకృత్ | The Creator of all beings |
| 6 | bhūtabhṛt | भूतभृत् | భూతభృత్ | The Sustainer of all beings |
| 7 | bhāvaḥ | भावः | భావః | Existence itself / Pure Being |
| 8 | bhūtātmā | भूतात्मा | భూతాత్మా | The Self (Ātman) of all beings |
| 9 | bhūtabhāvanaḥ | भूतभावनः | భూతభావనః | The Cause of all beings' becoming |

## The Discovery

88 characters. 80 linguistically valid decompositions. 3 to 9 names.

| Names | Readings | Character |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 3 | 1 | Maximally monistic — all functions collapsed |
| 4 | 7 | |
| 5 | 8 | |
| 6 | 13 | |
| 7 | 22 | Most ambiguity — the theological sweet spot |
| 8 | 7 | Seven ways to merge exactly one pair |
| **9** | **1** | **Unique maximum — every pair that CAN be split IS split** |

The text does not choose. The commentator chooses. The choice IS the theology.

- **9 names**: each divine function is distinct (Śaṅkara's vyāvahārika analysis)
- **7 names**: creation and sustenance are one act
- **3 names**: ALL functions of the divine are one unbroken reality (pure Advaita)

Same 88 characters. Same Sanskrit. Same verse. The grammar holds all readings simultaneously.
